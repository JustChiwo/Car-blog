Mercedes Benz E55 AMG

Unveiled at the Paris Motor Show in September 2002, the W211 E55 AMG debuted as the fastest production sedan in the world at the time. Autoevolution Under the hood sits a hand-built 5.4-litre supercharged V8 making 469 hp and 516 lb-ft of torque, paired to a 5-speed automatic and sending power to the rear wheels — capable of 0–60 in 4.5 seconds. MotorBiscuit The M113K engine won International Performance Engine of the Year for 2003. Interestingly, despite making slightly less power than the SL55 AMG due to its longer exhaust, Car and Driver's testing found the E55 was actually quicker. NetCarShow With more torque than both the BMW M5 (394 hp) and Audi RS6 (444 hp) of the era, it was a genuine force — even if the automatic gearbox made it more grand tourer than outright sports saloon. evo The E55 is widely regarded as having one of the most reliable Mercedes V8s ever built, which has only boosted its reputation in the enthusiast community over the years.
